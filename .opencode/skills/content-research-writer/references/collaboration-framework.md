## Citation Format Options

### Inline Citations (Best for: Narrative, web, blogs)

Maintains flow by integrating citations naturally:

```markdown
Studies show that remote teams are 20% more productive when given autonomy (McKinsey, 2024).

Or: According to the McKinsey Global Institute's 2024 study, remote teams are 20% more productive when given autonomy.

Or: Research indicates that autonomy drives productivity for remote teams [McKinsey Global Institute, 2024].
```

**Use when:** Story-driven or conversational writing; citations feel secondary to narrative

### Numbered Citations (Best for: Academic, long-form, formal)

Superscript numbers with comprehensive references at end:

```markdown
Remote teams show 20% higher productivity when given autonomy[1].

---

[1] McKinsey Global Institute. (2024). "The Future of Work: Remote Productivity and Autonomy." Retrieved from https://www.mckinsey.com/...
```

**Use when:** Academic standards, formal reports, or multiple detailed sources

### Footnote/Endnote Citations (Best for: Formal documents, papers)

Full citations in footer or end notes:

```markdown
Remote teams show 20% higher productivity when given autonomy.¹

---

¹ McKinsey Global Institute, "The Future of Work: Remote Productivity and Autonomy" (2024)
```

**Use when:** Scholarly work, formal documents, or complex citation needs

## Citation Consistency Audit Checklist

When preparing final draft:

**Format Consistency:**

- [ ] All citations use the same style (all inline, all numbered, all footnote)
- [ ] No mixed citation styles in same section
- [ ] Citation placement is consistent (e.g., always before period or after)

**Completeness:**

- [ ] Every claim that needs a source has one
- [ ] Every citation includes: Author, Year, Title (where applicable)
- [ ] Every citation has publication source

**Accuracy:**

- [ ] I've verified each source exists and is credible
- [ ] URLs (if included) are accurate and working
- [ ] Author names and publication dates are correct

**Format Details:**

- [ ] Author names spelled consistently
- [ ] Date format is consistent
- [ ] Publication titles are formatted the same way
- [ ] Punctuation around citations is uniform

**References List (if applicable):**

- [ ] All in-text citations are in the references list
- [ ] References list is alphabetized
- [ ] Format matches citation style throughout

## Collaboration Framework: Preserving Voice

### Learning Your Style

**Questions to ask early:**

- Can you share a writing sample you love? What makes it yours?
- What's your favorite writing? (Author, style, tone)
- What writing puts you off?
- How formal should this be?
- Any phrases or words you always use?

**Then:** Every suggestion I make matches your voice, not my preference.

### During Feedback

**Preserve voice by:**

- Offering alternatives, not prescriptions
- Suggesting edits that keep your word choices
- Asking "Does this sound like you?" before finalizing suggestions
- Learning your pace and rhythm; don't flatten them

**Red flags:**

- Your edits sound nothing like the original writer
- You've replaced idiosyncratic language with generic alternatives
- The final piece reads like corporate templates

### Respecting Choices

- If writer prefers their version over my suggestion: Support it
- If writer rejects feedback: Don't revisit it in next section
- If writer has a personal style preference: Adapt all feedback to match

---

**Bottom Line:** Your voice is the asset. I make it clearer and more credible, not different.
