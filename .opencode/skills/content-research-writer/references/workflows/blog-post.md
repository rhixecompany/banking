# Blog Post Workflow

## Timeline: 1-2 weeks (with feedback cycles)

## Phase 1: Planning (30 min)

**Step 1: Clarify the angle**

- What's the core argument or insight you want to share?
- Who are you writing for?
- What problem does this post solve for them?
- What will they know/feel differently after reading?

**Step 2: Outline together**

- Hook/opening (compelling question, story, or stat)
- 3-4 main sections (building argument or teaching concept)
- Conclusion (summary + call to action or next step)

**Step 3: Identify research needs**

- What data would strengthen this?
- Any expert quotes or examples needed?
- Sources you already have vs. need to find

## Phase 2: Research (1-2 hours)

**Step 1: Find sources**

- Blog posts, studies, tweets, customer examples
- Prioritize recent, credible sources
- Lean on personal experience + research (not just links)

**Step 2: Extract key findings**

- 3-5 key points from research
- Direct quotes if strong
- Examples that illustrate your point

**Step 3: Review together**

- Confirm research supports your thesis
- Discard anything that dilutes focus
- Note where research fits in outline

## Phase 3: Writing (2-3 hours spread across 3 days)

**Day 1 — Hook + First Section:**

- Write compelling hook/intro (150-200 words)
- Complete first section (300-400 words)
- Share for feedback

**Feedback Cycle:**

- I review for clarity, flow, and whether examples land
- Suggest improvements, offer alternatives
- You revise based on what resonates

**Day 2 — Sections 2-3:**

- Write sections 2 and 3 (300-400 words each)
- Each section should build on previous insight
- Share sections independently for targeted feedback

**Day 3 — Conclusion + Polish:**

- Write conclusion that summarizes + calls to action
- Read entire draft aloud
- Share for full review

## Phase 4: Revise (1-2 hours)

**Step 1: Review feedback**

- Focus on structure and flow feedback first
- Then tackle clarity and evidence gaps
- Polish comes last

**Step 2: Revise strategically**

- Major issues first (structure, evidence)
- Then clarity (word choice, flow)
- Then style (polish, consistency)

**Step 3: Final check**

- Read aloud one more time
- Check: hook strong? conclusion lands? CTА clear?
- Verify all claims supported

## Phase 5: Publish (1 hour)

**Before publishing:**

- [ ] Proofread for typos and grammar
- [ ] Format: headers, bold, lists clear
- [ ] All links working
- [ ] Featured image ready
- [ ] Meta description written

**Publishing:**

- Add to CMS/blog platform
- Social media post draft
- Internal sharing plan

## Tips for Blog Success

- **Voice matters** — Blog readers want authentic perspective, not corporate tone
- **Examples are oxygen** — Generic claims bore; specific examples engage
- **Link generously** — Show your sources; gives reader confidence + SEO benefit
- **Call to action** — Don't leave readers hanging; tell them what's next
- **Deadline helps** — "I want to publish this Thursday" creates focus

## What Feedback Looks Like (Blog-Specific)

**For blog posts, I focus on:**

- Does the hook grab you in first 2 sentences?
- Is the core argument clear by end of section 1?
- Do examples feel specific and true?
- Is the voice consistent and authentic?
- Does conclusion drive action?
- Is this shareable? Would you tweet this?
